# Aesthetic Routing

The card's whole visual register is chosen BY THE SUBJECT, automatically — the user does not
pick a style. Ranking ancient Chinese poets must look like ink-wash literati art, not a
duotone sports card. Route first, then apply the harness inside that register.

This is the fix for the #1 failure of ranking cards: defaulting every subject to the
modern-sports look. The duotone-sports register is ONE route, not the default.

## How to route

1. Read the subject. Identify era + culture + field (ancient vs modern, Chinese vs Western,
   person vs institution vs object).
2. Pick the register from the table. When two fit, pick the one the audience would expect to
   see this subject in (poets belong on rice paper, not a stadium).
3. Apply that register's tokens to the WHOLE card — ground, portrait treatment, tier-label
   style, accents, type. The harness (one pipeline, stated basis, bounded tiers) runs INSIDE
   the register.
4. The user may override ("做成赛博朋克风的诗人榜"), but the default is the routed register,
   never the sports card.

### Tie-break & fallthrough

- **Tie-break is by era + culture + field, in that order** — not by vibe. An ancient Chinese
  subject routes ink-wash even inside a 中外混排 card. If a card mixes eras/cultures that cannot
  share one register (e.g. 李白 + 莎士比亚), do NOT blend: either split into two cards, or
  convert ALL subjects to one neutral unifying register (e.g. all toned engraving) and state
  that choice. Never let the modern-sports look win by default.
- **Fallthrough (subject not in the table):** route to 编辑级肖像 + 网格 (Platon face-on-void +
  Swiss grid) for people, and 现代信息图 (flat icon system) for no-face subjects — these are the
  safe neutral defaults. The duotone-sports register is NEVER the fallthrough; it is only for
  the explicit 现代/当代球星 rows.

## The ink-wash register (most-requested, often missed)

For 古代诗人/词人/书画家, default to:

- **Ground:** warm rice-paper (宣纸) texture, soft and tactile.
- **Portrait:** ink-wash (水墨) or fine-line (工笔) portrait; all subjects in one ink register,
  unified paper tone. Never a photoreal or duotone face.
- **Layout:** a hanging-scroll (卷轴) or album-leaf (册页) feel; vertical columns; lots of 留白.
- **Tier labels:** vertical 书法 (brush calligraphy); classical grades 神品/逸品/妙品/能品 read
  far better than S/A/B/C and carry real cultural weight.
- **Accents:** red 印章 (seal stamps) for the name or rank — the one warm note in an ink field.
- **Type:** a 书法/宋体 register, vertical where it fits; the basis line in a quiet 小楷.
- **Color:** ink black + paper + one seal-red. No warm→cool tier ramp; rank reads by position
  and 品级, not by traffic-light color.

This register draws on Chinese ink aesthetics (宋元山水, 文人画, 书法, 留白/计白当黑) — see the
East-Asian section of the master library for the transferable tokens.
