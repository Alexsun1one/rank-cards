# Domain Adaptation

The harness — bounded tiers, a stated basis, one unified portrait pipeline, warm→cool color,
clear typography — is domain-invariant. Only three adapters change per domain: the
**portrait**, the **prop / affiliation**, and the **criteria**. The act of stating the basis
and unifying the portraits never changes.

## Adapter notes

- **Pre-photography domains** (writers, classical musicians, scientists) often have only
  paintings or engravings. The pipeline's "one art register" rule means: pick all-painting OR
  convert everything to one unified tone. Never mix an oil portrait with a modern photo.
- **The prop generalizes** from jersey → logo / instrument / book cover / film motif. It is
  the secondary recognizability cue; keep its treatment consistent too.
- **The criteria shift** from trophies/数据 (sports) to 影响力/开创性/流传度 (culture) — but
  printing the basis is equally mandatory in both.
- **Pick era-appropriate affiliation**: a footballer's peak club, a writer's defining work, a
  director's signature film. Hold the choice across the whole card.
- **No-face subjects** (schools, universities, majors, cities, products, shows): there is no
  portrait. The portrait pipeline becomes a **subject-image pipeline** — unify the
  logo / landmark / icon / render with the SAME crop, frame, treatment, and art register for
  every entry. A row of crests must all be the same size, on the same backdrop, in one style;
  a row of city skylines must share one crop and one duotone. The five locks still apply; the
  "face" is just a logo or landmark now.
- **Icon-only rankings** (majors, abstract categories): commission ONE icon set in one weight
  and style; never mix a flat icon with a 3D render with a photo.

## The mapping in one line

> Swap three adapters — portrait, prop, criteria — and the same harness ranks anything you can
> compare: players, poets, schools, majors, cities, films, products.
